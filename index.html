What STEPS does

The STEPS FETP India Decision Aid combines a mixed logit preference model with costing and epidemiological calculations to support planning of Field Epidemiology Training Programmes in India.

It uses the preference model to predict endorsement and willingness to pay for different programme configurations, applies harmonised cost components for each tier, and links these to epidemiological assumptions about graduates and outbreak responses.

The opt out alternative represents the situation where no new FETP training is funded and is used as the benchmark.

How to use this tool

Start in the Configuration tab to choose programme features, cohort size and planning horizon. Apply the configuration to generate endorsement, costs and outbreak related benefits. Use the National view and Sensitivity tabs to examine aggregate results and key assumptions before preparing a policy brief in the Copilot tab.
