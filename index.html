What STEPS does

STEPS combines preference study results, cost templates and epidemiological assumptions to help ministries of health, finance and partners assess different Field Epidemiology Training Programme configurations.

The tool runs locally in the browser. No personal data are collected and no scenario inputs are sent to external servers.

Quick start for senior officials

  1. Go to the Configure scenario tab and either choose a policy question preset or adjust the tier, career incentive, mentorship, delivery mode and cohort parameters.
  2. Select Apply configuration to update endorsement, costs, epidemiological benefits and national level indicators.
  3. Use the Results and National scale view tabs to review key numbers and charts, then export Excel or PDF summaries for meetings and reports.

Policy question presets

These presets help frame the analysis around typical questions ministries ask. Each preset can pre-fill the configuration form and highlight relevant outputs.

After selecting a preset, move to the Configure scenario tab to review or adjust the details before applying the configuration.

Scenario templates

STEPS can store template scenarios that illustrate typical policy options.

  • Conservative scale up - smaller cohort size and fewer cohorts with strong mentorship.
  • Aggressive scale up - larger cohort size and more cohorts with blended delivery.
  • Mentorship focused - emphasis on higher mentorship intensity within a given budget.
  • Budget constrained - configurations that respect a predefined total cost ceiling.

These templates are accessible in the Scenario library and comparison tab and can be adapted to country specific needs.

This tab summarises the main data sources, methods and limitations so that technical reviewers and economists can assess the approach.

Methods overview

Preference model

The preference model is a mixed logit estimated from a discrete choice experiment conducted with stakeholders involved in FETP planning. It predicts endorsement and opt out rates and generates willingness to pay values in Indian rupees.

Costing

Cost templates combine salary and benefits, travel, training materials, trainee support and other direct and indirect items, including the opportunity cost of trainee time where this option is switched on.

Epidemiological module

Epidemiological outputs are based on completion rates, outbreaks handled per graduate per year, the assumed value per outbreak response, the planning horizon and the epidemiological discount rate. Response time affects a multiplier applied to outbreak responses.

Limitations

The model is designed as a structured decision aid. It uses simplified assumptions and indicative values, so results should be interpreted as order of magnitude estimates rather than exact forecasts.

About STEPS

            

            

                

                    STEPS is a decision aid for ministries of health and finance, partners and technical agencies that are planning investments in Field Epidemiology Training Programmes.
                

                

                    The logo is the STEPS mark represented by the letter S in the header circle. It is used consistently across tabs for recognition.
                

            

        


        

            

                
Privacy and security

            

            

                

                    The tool runs in the browser and does not collect or transmit personal data. Scenario inputs and outputs remain on the device unless users explicitly export and share them.
                

                

                    If the tool is later connected to a server for scenario storage, role based access and audit logs should be implemented so that users can be assigned viewer, analyst or administrator roles.

Help and frequently asked questions

        
    

    

        

            
What is endorsement?

            

                Endorsement is the predicted share of decision makers in the preference study who choose the FETP option rather than the opt out alternative for the current configuration.
            

        

        

            
How should I interpret the benefit cost ratio?

            

                A benefit cost ratio above one indicates that estimated benefits exceed economic costs under the current assumptions. Values between one and one point two can be considered borderline and may warrant further sensitivity checks.
            

        

        

            
When should I include the opportunity cost of trainee time?

            

                Include opportunity cost when comparing FETP investments with other uses of funds from an economic perspective. Excluding it may be appropriate for some financial budgeting exercises where only direct programme costs are considered.
            

        

        

            
How do I use STEPS in meetings?

            

                Use scenario templates and the scenario library to prepare a small set of options in advance. During meetings, show the comparison tables, headline indicators and charts, and export the results to Excel or PDF for circulation afterwards.
